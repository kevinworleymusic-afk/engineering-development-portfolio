## Mission

Develop the knowledge, practical skills, engineering mindset, and professional portfolio needed for entry-level engineering positions—beginning with acoustical consulting while preserving specialization paths into automotive audio and aerospace.

## Program Structure

Each module combines five parts:

1. **Mathematics** — the mathematical tools required for engineering.
2. **Core Engineering** — acoustics, measurement, DSP, vibrations, wave propagation, electroacoustics, simulation, and professional consulting.
3. **Engineering Projects** — practical work using Python, MATLAB, measurements, REW, simulations, and technical reporting.
4. **Career Forks** — optional specialization labs in automotive audio and aerospace.
5. **Engineering Case Study** — a realistic conceptual scenario requiring problem definition, analysis, recommendations, and professional communication.

## Learning Philosophy

This program is competency-based rather than calendar-based. A module is complete when its concepts can be explained, its engineering problems can be solved, its measurements or analyses can be performed, and its results can be communicated through portfolio-quality work.

## Portfolio Goals

Major modules will produce polished work such as:

- Room-acoustics and RT60 studies
- Loudspeaker and vehicle-cabin measurements
- Classroom and lecture-hall designs
- Python and MATLAB engineering tools
- DSP implementations and simulations
- Engineering case studies and professional reports
