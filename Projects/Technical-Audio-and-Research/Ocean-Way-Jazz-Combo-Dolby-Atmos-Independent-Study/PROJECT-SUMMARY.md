# Ocean Way Jazz Combo: Dolby Atmos Recording Independent Study

## Project overview

This collaborative independent study investigated the use of a 2L Cube-inspired microphone array for immersive music recording in a 7.1.4 Dolby Atmos configuration.

A jazz combo and string ensemble were recorded in the historic Ocean Way Nashville Studio A using its world-class commercial recording environment, acoustics, and professional equipment.

## Research team

- Kevin Worley

- George Lawson

## Technical approach

The project emphasized creating the immersive image during recording rather than relying on extensive moving-object automation.

The workflow used:

- A 2L Cube-inspired main microphone array
- Seven horizontal and four height positions
- Fixed microphone-to-speaker routing
- Instrument spot microphones
- Direct inputs for bass and guitar
- The natural acoustics of the historic Studio A
- Separate management of the LFE path

## Surviving evidence

Two Pro Tools sessions survive:

- `031224_Mix1_Four.ptx`
- `032024_Mix1_Afteryouvegone.ptx`

Recoverable session information confirms 2L Cube, front, rear, and height groups; eleven full-range output positions; jazz-combo and string instrumentation; spot microphones; reverbs; and object-routing elements.

A surviving front-right-height export is a synchronized 48 kHz, 24-bit mono WAV created in Pro Tools.
