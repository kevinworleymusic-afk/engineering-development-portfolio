# Recording Methodology

## Objective

Investigate whether a 2L Cube-inspired array could capture a jazz combo and string ensemble as a stable, natural immersive soundstage for 7.1.4 Dolby Atmos reproduction.

## Recording location

The project was conducted in the historic Ocean Way Nashville Studio A. The commercial facility provided a world-class acoustic environment and access to professional microphones, signal paths, monitoring, and recording infrastructure.

The precise microphone models are not available in the surviving documentation.

## Main array

The main recording approach was informed by Morten Lindberg’s 2L Cube technique.

The array captured eleven full-range spatial positions:

- Left, center, and right
- Left and right side-surround positions
- Left and right rear-surround positions
- Front-left and front-right height positions
- Rear-left and rear-right height positions

The LFE path completing the 7.1.4 configuration was managed separately rather than captured by a dedicated array microphone.

## Ensemble and supporting capture

The surviving sessions identify:

- Four violins
- Viola
- Cello
- Drums
- Kick microphone
- Overhead microphones
- Double-bass spot microphone
- Bass DI
- Guitar microphone
- Guitar DI

These sources supplemented the main immersive array and provided additional control over instrumental balance.

## Spatial approach

The project emphasized preserving the spatial relationships captured in the room. Array positions were routed to their corresponding reproduction positions, creating a stable immersive image.

Extensive moving-object automation was not central to the workflow. Performer placement, microphone geometry, fixed routing, spot-microphone balance, and the acoustics of the historic Studio A established the presentation.
