# Results Summary

## Overview

The Studer A80 hardware recorder and Universal Audio Studer A800 plug-in were compared in input mode and at 15 and 30 inches per second (IPS).

The comparison evaluated:

- Frequency response
- Signal-to-noise ratio (SNR)
- Total harmonic distortion plus noise (THD+N)

The systems produced similar results under some conditions but showed meaningful differences at both tape-speed settings.

## Numerical results

| Mode | Hardware SNR | Plug-in SNR | Difference | Hardware THD+N | Plug-in THD+N |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Input | -68.85 dBrA | -68.1 dBrA | 0.75 dB | 0.08% | 0.074% |
| 15 IPS | -60.3 dBrA | -67.8 dBrA | 7.5 dB | 0.26% | 0.26% |
| 30 IPS | -62.5 dBrA | -67.0 dBrA | 4.5 dB | 0.20% | 0.54% |

## Input mode

### Frequency response

The input-mode frequency responses were nearly identical. The hardware response exhibited an approximately 1 dB high-frequency reduction between roughly 8 and 20 kHz that was not present in the plug-in response.

Because input mode does not include the tape-recording process, both systems would ideally remain flat. The plug-in more closely maintained that expected flat response.

### SNR and THD+N

The input-mode measurements closely agreed:

- SNR differed by 0.75 dB.
- THD+N measured 0.08% for the hardware and 0.074% for the plug-in.

Input mode produced the closest overall agreement between the two systems.

## 15 IPS

### Frequency response

The largest frequency-response difference occurred at 15 IPS.

Below approximately 100 Hz:

- The plug-in produced a low-frequency rise approaching 2 dB.
- The hardware measurement showed a reduction approaching 3 dB.

Smaller differences also appeared from approximately 200 Hz to 1 kHz and from 2 to 10 kHz. The magnitude and opposite direction of the low-frequency responses made this the study’s most consequential frequency-response deviation.

### SNR and THD+N

THD+N matched exactly at 0.26% for both systems.

The reported SNR values differed by 7.5 dB:

- Hardware: -60.3 dBrA
- Plug-in: -67.8 dBrA

The more negative plug-in measurement indicated a lower reported noise level relative to the reference used in the study.

## 30 IPS

### Frequency response

The 30 IPS frequency responses generally remained within approximately 0.5 dB.

The main differences were:

- Between approximately 100 and 500 Hz, the plug-in response decreased by about 0.5 dB while the hardware remained comparatively flat.
- Between approximately 1 and 8 kHz, the hardware response increased by about 0.25 dB while the plug-in remained comparatively flat.

These differences were measurable but smaller than the low-frequency disagreement at 15 IPS.

### SNR and THD+N

The reported SNR values differed by 4.5 dB:

- Hardware: -62.5 dBrA
- Plug-in: -67.0 dBrA

The largest distortion disagreement occurred at this speed:

- Hardware THD+N: 0.20%
- Plug-in THD+N: 0.54%

The plug-in measurement was 0.34 percentage points higher and approximately 2.7 times the hardware value.

## Interpretation

The plug-in did not produce a uniformly exact reproduction of the tested Studer A80. Its level of agreement depended on the operating condition and measurement being considered.

The strongest agreements were:

- Input-mode frequency response
- Input-mode SNR and THD+N
- THD+N at 15 IPS
- Most of the 30 IPS frequency response

The most important disagreements were:

- Low-frequency behavior at 15 IPS
- SNR at 15 IPS
- THD+N at 30 IPS

The plug-in’s SNR measurements remained relatively consistent across all three modes, while the analog machine varied more substantially. This suggests that the plug-in reproduced some characteristics of the hardware while retaining the operational consistency expected from a digital system.

## Limitations

These results apply to the specific Studer A80, tape reel, calibration, plug-in configuration, interface, and measurement system used in the experiment.

No repeated-trial statistics or formal uncertainty bounds were calculated. The measurements should therefore be interpreted as an engineering comparison of the tested systems rather than proof of how every Studer A80 or tape-emulation plug-in will perform.
