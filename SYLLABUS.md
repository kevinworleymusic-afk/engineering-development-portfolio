# Engineering Development Program Syllabus

## A Personalized Curriculum in Acoustical Engineering, DSP, and Applied Engineering

## Mission

Develop the knowledge, practical skills, engineering mindset, and professional portfolio needed for entry-level engineering positions, beginning with acoustical consulting while preserving specialization paths into automotive audio and potentially aerospace.

## Curriculum Philosophy

This is not simply a collection of lessons. The purpose is to learn to think like an engineer.

Every module combines:

- Mathematics
- Engineering theory
- Hands-on projects
- Career specialization
- Realistic conceptual engineering case studies

## Standard Module Structure

### Part I: Mathematics

Develop the mathematical tools required for engineering.

Topics may include:

- Functions
- Logarithms
- Trigonometry
- Complex numbers
- Calculus
- Differential equations

### Part II: Core Engineering

Learn the engineering concepts supported by the mathematics.

Topics may include:

- Physics of sound
- Architectural acoustics
- Engineering measurement
- Digital signal processing
- Vibrations
- Wave propagation
- Electroacoustics
- Simulation
- Professional consulting

### Part III: Engineering Projects

Apply the material through practical work, including:

- Python tools
- MATLAB programs
- Acoustic measurements
- RT60 analysis
- REW projects
- Loudspeaker measurements
- DSP implementations
- Engineering reports

Every major project should be developed toward eventual professional-portfolio quality.

### Part IV: Career Forks

Optional specialization labs connect the common engineering foundation to potential career paths.

#### Automotive Audio

- Vehicle-cabin acoustics
- Speaker placement
- DSP tuning
- Active noise control
- Spatial audio
- Premium automotive sound systems

#### Aerospace

- Sensors
- Vibrations
- Instrumentation
- Control systems
- Systems engineering
- Telemetry

### Part V: Engineering Case Study

Every module ends with a realistic engineering scenario.

Examples include:

- Improving speech intelligibility in a classroom
- Designing a lecture hall within a $15,000 budget
- Evaluating a vehicle cabin
- Diagnosing poor room acoustics
- Investigating abnormal vibration measurements

Each case study requires the learner to:

1. Identify and define the problem.
2. Request or document missing information.
3. State assumptions and limitations.
4. Perform an appropriate analysis.
5. Compare feasible options.
6. Make technically justified recommendations.
7. Communicate the work in a professional engineering report.

These are conceptual educational case studies rather than representations of commissioned consulting work. Portfolio materials must label them honestly.

## Deliverables for Every Module

- Lesson notes
- Guided notes
- Practice problems
- Quiz
- Laboratory exercise
- Programming project
- Engineering report
- Optional automotive lab
- Optional aerospace lab
- Competency test
- Answer key

Some deliverables may be combined when that produces a more coherent learning experience, but each competency must still be demonstrated.

## Portfolio Philosophy

The curriculum simultaneously builds an engineering portfolio. Major modules should produce at least one polished artifact rather than only disposable homework.

Possible portfolio pieces include:

- Room-acoustics reports
- RT60 studies
- Loudspeaker measurements
- Classroom designs
- Lecture-hall analyses
- Vehicle-cabin evaluations
- Python engineering tools
- DSP implementations
- Engineering simulations
- Professional-style consulting reports

Drafts, exercises, answer keys, and polished portfolio artifacts should remain distinguishable.

## Competency-Based Progression

Progress is based on demonstrated competency rather than a fixed calendar.

A module is complete when the learner can:

- Explain its central concepts in plain and technical language
- Solve representative engineering problems
- Perform the relevant measurements, calculations, or simulations
- Interpret results and recognize limitations
- Communicate the reasoning and recommendations
- Demonstrate practical application through a project or case study

A score alone does not establish mastery. Incorrect work should be diagnosed, revised, and retested.

## Long-Term Goal

By the end of the curriculum, the learner should have:

- A solid engineering foundation
- Strong mathematical intuition
- Practical measurement experience
- Programming projects in Python and later MATLAB or C++
- Professional technical-writing experience
- A GitHub portfolio of engineering projects
- A collection of clearly labeled conceptual engineering case studies
- Skills applicable to acoustical consulting, with pathways into automotive audio and other engineering specialties
